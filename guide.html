The Three-Layer Cost Model

Most TCO tools only show direct cost — what you paid in salary for unproductive time. This calculator adds two more layers to show the full organizational impact. Every cost output uses this same consistent structure.

Direct Cost

What you paid for unproductive time. Salary and benefits still going out the door.

Loaded Hourly × Wasted Hours
+
Opportunity Cost

The additional value lost beyond compensation — the delta between what you paid and what that hour would have generated.

(Value/Hr − Loaded Hourly) × Wasted Hours
=
Total Impact

The full value that hour would have generated for the organization.

Value/Hr × Wasted Hours

How Each Tab Works

🔄

Recurring Issue Cost

Tab 1

Inputs Needed

  • Employee role and salary (the person affected)
  • Active time + hold/wait time per incident
  • Support tier and salary
  • Support resolution time
  • Annual ticket volume
  • Optional: Hidden cost factors (workarounds, repeat contacts, error/rework, escalations, overtime)

When to Use

Quantifying the true cost of recurring IT issues — password resets, system access problems, manual processes, application errors.

What It Calculates

  • Per-ticket cost (three-layer breakdown)
  • Annual cost at current volume
  • Sensitivity analysis (savings at various reduction %)
  • Hidden cost factor breakdown

Key Formula

Per-Ticket = (Customer Hours × Value/Hr)
  + (Support Hours × Value/Hr × Repeat × Multi-Touch)
  + Resource Cost + Hidden Factors

Annual Cost = Per-Ticket × Annual Volume
📈

Improvement ROI

Tab 2

Inputs Needed

  • Current annual cost of the problem
  • Expected improvement percentage
  • Confidence level (conservative / moderate / aggressive)
  • One-time investment cost
  • Annual recurring cost of the solution

When to Use

Building a business case for a proposed solution. Feeds directly from Tab 1's annual cost output.

What It Calculates

  • Confidence-adjusted annual savings
  • Payback period in months
  • ROI % at 1, 2, 3, and 5 years
  • Net Present Value (NPV) at each horizon

Key Formula

Savings = Annual Cost × Improvement% × Confidence
Net Benefit = Savings − Recurring Cost
Payback = Investment ÷ (Net Benefit / 12) months
NPV = −Investment + ∑(Benefit / (1 + r)year)
🏗️

Implementation TCO

Tab 3

Inputs Needed

  • Software/hardware costs (one-time)
  • Implementation services
  • Internal staff hours and salary
  • Training hours per person, number of trainees
  • Productivity dip %, transition weeks, affected employees
  • Recurring licensing and support costs

When to Use

Evaluating the true cost of a new system or major change — beyond the vendor's sticker price.

What It Calculates

  • Year 1 total cost of ownership
  • 5-year total cost of ownership
  • Hidden cost ratio (% of costs typically overlooked)
  • Breakdown: direct costs vs. labor vs. training vs. productivity loss

Key Formula

Year 1 TCO = Direct Costs + Internal Labor
  + Training Cost + Productivity Loss
5-Year TCO = Year 1 + (Ongoing Annual × 4)
Hidden Cost % = (Training + Prod. Loss) / Year 1
⚡

Downtime Cost

Tab 4

Inputs Needed

  • Number of affected employees
  • Average salary of affected staff
  • Downtime duration in hours
  • Revenue impact percentage
  • Recovery costs (overtime, emergency vendor, etc.)
  • Expected events per year

When to Use

Quantifying outage impact for incident response planning, disaster recovery investment, or SLA negotiations.

What It Calculates

  • Per-event cost (conservative and full impact)
  • Lost productivity component
  • Lost revenue component
  • Annualized downtime cost

Key Formula

Event Cost = (Employees × Hourly Rate × Hours)
  + Lost Revenue + Recovery Costs
Annual = Event Cost × Events per Year
⚖️

Compare Options

Tab 5

Inputs Needed

  • Baseline scenario (current state costs)
  • Up to 3 alternative scenarios
  • Per-ticket time and cost for each option
  • Annual ticket volume

When to Use

Choosing between competing solutions, vendors, or approaches with a side-by-side cost comparison.

What It Calculates

  • Side-by-side annual cost comparison
  • Net difference from baseline for each option
  • Annual savings per alternative
  • Best value indicator

Key Formula

Option Cost = Per-Ticket Cost × Annual Volume
Savings vs. Baseline = Baseline Cost − Option Cost

Formula Equation What It Tells You
Per-Ticket CostCustomer Impact + Support Impact + Resources + Hidden FactorsTotal cost of a single incident
Annual CostPer-Ticket × Annual VolumeYearly burden of a recurring issue

Formula Equation What It Tells You

What Makes This Different

Typical TCO Calculator This Calculator
Only shows direct costs (salary paid for idle time) Three-layer cost model: Direct Cost + Opportunity Cost = Total Impact. Shows both conservative and full-impact numbers side by side.
Ignores what happens outside the help desk ticket Models six hidden cost factors: workaround labor, repeat contacts, error/rework, management overhead, overtime/backlog, and context switching.
Produces a single point estimate Confidence-adjusted estimates (conservative / moderate / aggressive) so you can present numbers you trust to any audience.
Uses arbitrary cost multipliers or flat hourly rates Revenue-based value/hr grounded in organizational data (revenue per employee × utilization), with a fallback multiplier when revenue data is unavailable.
Accepts the vendor's sticker price as the total cost Explicitly calculates and exposes the hidden cost ratio — training, productivity loss, and transition costs that typically account for 40–60% of implementation cost.
